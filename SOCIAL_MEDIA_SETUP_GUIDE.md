### Content Pillars

1. **Science & Research** (30%)
   - Latest restoration techniques
   - Research findings
   - Member spotlights

2. **Community & Events** (30%)
   - ICSR2026 updates
   - Member stories
   - Field trip highlights

3. **Education & Impact** (25%)
   - Why shellfish matter
   - Success stories
   - Ecosystem services

4. **Calls to Action** (15%)
   - Conference registration
   - Membership
   - Donations

### Posting Frequency
- **LinkedIn:** 3-4 posts per week
- **Facebook:** 4-5 posts per week (can be more casual/visual)

### Best Times to Post
- **LinkedIn:** Tuesday-Thursday, 9am-11am or 1pm-3pm (EST)
- **Facebook:** Wednesday-Friday, 12pm-2pm or 7pm-9pm (EST)

## PART 5: BRAND VOICE & STYLE GUIDE

### Voice Characteristics
- **Professional yet Accessible:** Scientific credibility without jargon
- **Collaborative:** Emphasize community and partnership
- **Solution-Oriented:** Focus on impact and success
- **Passionate:** Show enthusiasm for the mission
- **Global:** Highlight international scope

### Writing Style
- Use active voice
- Keep sentences concise
- Include emojis sparingly on Facebook, minimally on LinkedIn
- Always include clear calls to action
- Tag partners and collaborators when relevant

### Visual Style
- Use high-quality photos of shellfish, restoration work, and conferences
- Include people in photos when possible
- Maintain consistent logo placement
- Use ISRS brand colors: Green (#2c5f2d) and Blue (#003d82)

## PART 6: ENGAGEMENT GUIDELINES

### Responding to Comments
- Respond within 24 hours
- Thank supporters and donors
- Answer questions thoroughly
- Direct technical questions to: aaron@shellfish-society.org
- Never delete critical comments unless they violate community standards

### Community Standards
- No spam or promotional content
- Respectful scientific discourse encouraged
- No hate speech or harassment
- Keep discussions relevant to shellfish restoration

### Moderation
- Review comments daily
- Hide (don't delete) spam
- Report serious violations
- Document patterns for future reference
